Chidambaram

"Atmosphere of Wisdom" — Derived from Chit (Consciousness) & Ambaram (Sky/Space)

Chidambaram is a historic temple town located in the Cuddalore district of Tamil Nadu, India. The town grew in concentric circles around its spiritual center and is globally renowned for its deep cultural, architectural, and religious heritage.

The spiritual heart of the town, dedicated to Lord Shiva as the cosmic dancer (Nataraja) performing the Ananda Tandavam (Dance of Bliss). Unlike most Shiva temples which feature a Lingam, here Lord Shiva is represented in his anthropomorphic form dancing.
It is one of the five holiest Shiva temples (Pancha Bhoota Sthalams), uniquely representing the element of Akasha (Ether/Space). The famous "Chidambaram Rahasyam" (secret) lies here, symbolizing that God is formless and omnipresent like space.
Historically an ancient capital, Chidambaram flourished immensely under the royal patronage of great south Indian dynasties including the Pallavas, Cholas, Pandyas, and the Vijayanagara Kings, who added magnificent gold-roofed structures to the sanctum.
Famous for hosting the annual Natyanjali Dance Festival during Maha Shivaratri. Classical dancers from all across the globe converge here to pay their artistic tribute to Lord Nataraja, the ultimate lord of dance.
